# Audacion AI Labs — Competitive Salary Guide for All 19 PRISM Roles
### Los Angeles & San Francisco Benchmarks | 2026 Edition

***

## Executive Overview

This report benchmarks competitive salaries for all 19 roles across Audacion AI Labs' PRISM Research Framework — covering Research, Technology, Community & Fellowship, Operations & Growth, and Support pillars. Because the mission is to attract the best talent in a field where industry labs pay $300K–$900K for technical staff, this guide recommends using **San Francisco Bay Area benchmarks** as the offer ceiling for every AI/technical role and **Los Angeles market rates** for operations/communications/support roles, whichever yields the higher figure. This hybrid approach maximizes competitiveness without uniformly inflating the entire payroll.[^1][^2]

**Key framing note:** Audacion AI Labs is a grant-funded research operation. The salary ranges recommended here reflect what a funded, mission-driven AI lab should offer to compete with academia, think tanks, and smaller AI-focused nonprofits — not frontier labs like Anthropic or OpenAI whose compensation structures include equity that is not applicable in this context. Offering at the 75th percentile of the nonprofit/think tank benchmark for each category is the recommended posture.

***

## Methodology

Salary data was sourced from ZipRecruiter, Salary.com, Glassdoor, Built In, Indeed, PayScale, Comparably, Levels.fyi, Robert Half, and the AI Safety Career Guide (2025–2026). For each role, the **recommended range** reflects:
- The 25th–75th percentile of comparable roles in Los Angeles or San Francisco (whichever is higher)
- Adjustments for the AI/research context, grant-funded environment, and mission-driven positioning
- A separate "stretch" figure at the 90th percentile to represent top-of-market for exceptional candidates

## Research Roles

The Director of Research is the most senior scientific role and the equivalent of a VP of Research at a think tank or a Research Director at an AI safety organization. ## Strategic Positioning Guidance

### Why San Francisco Benchmarks for Technical & Research Roles
San Francisco is the highest-paying market for AI safety researchers ($206,000 median vs. $195,000 California average), lead software engineers ($204,797 Salary.com vs. $157,198 LA), and data engineers ($171,450–$244,013 vs. $164,733–$235,145 LA). Using SF as the anchor for these roles signals that Audacion AI Labs is serious about competing for talent that has offers from Bay Area labs.[^14][^18][^15][^19][^6]

### Why LA Benchmarks for Operations, Communications, and Support
For roles where geography matters less and where LA's media/entertainment ecosystem provides a talent pipeline — PR, communications, EA, operations — LA rates are competitive and appropriate. The LA market is itself well above national averages: Operations Managers average $123,794, PR Managers average $103,675–$143,700, and Community Directors average $101,333.[^21][^27][^38][^39]

### The Nonprofit Positioning Reality
Think tank senior fellows and managers — the closest analog to Pillar Research Leads in a funded nonprofit research context — earn $120,000–$180,000, and junior think tank researchers earn $70,000–$100,000. This report's recommended ranges sit above those figures intentionally. Audacion AI Labs' citizen science platform, AI-augmentation layer, and direct publication mission position it closer to a funded AI research institute than a traditional think tank, justifying the premium.[^5]

### Grant Budget Planning
When drafting NSF SBIR, Cartier, or CalOSBA applications, plan for **full-time equivalent (FTE) salary figures at the midpoints** listed in the table above, plus a standard **25–30% benefits and overhead multiplier**. For example, a Director of Research at $190,000 midpoint budgets to approximately $237,500–$247,000 in total FTE cost to a funder. Grantors expect to see market-justified compensation and reject budgets that appear below-market as a signal of operational immaturity.
