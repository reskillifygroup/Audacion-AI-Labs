# Audacion AI Labs — Master Funding Guide
## Legal Structure Analysis, Grant Database & $10M Annual Funding Strategy

***

## Executive Overview

This report answers two foundational questions for Audacion AI Labs: what legal structure maximizes funding access, and what is the full universe of grants, corporate funds, government programs, and identity-based opportunities available to the lab. The goal of $10 million in annual funding is achievable — but it requires the right legal architecture, a diversified funding stack, and sequential execution across at least three to five funding streams simultaneously.

The most important structural insight: **a Delaware Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) — the Anthropic model — paired with a 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor for grant eligibility is the optimal configuration for a lab at this stage.** Neither a pure nonprofit nor a pure for-profit alone will maximize access to all available funding. The hybrid approach does.

## Part 1: Legal Structure — The Anthropic/OpenAI Model and What It Means for Dee

### What Anthropic Actually Is

Anthropic is incorporated as a **Delaware Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)**. Its certificate of incorporation explicitly states its purpose: "to responsibly develop and maintain advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity". It is **not a nonprofit** — Anthropic does not file public 990s with the IRS, does not have 501(c)(3) status, and is under no obligation to accept unsolicited grant proposals. Its governance is reinforced by the Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT), which controls board composition over time.[^1][^2][^3]

OpenAI has now adopted a similar structure: the nonprofit (OpenAI Foundation) retains control and a major equity stake in the for-profit subsidiary, which just completed its conversion to a PBC (OpenAI Group PBC). Both companies validate that the PBC is the dominant legal model for serious, mission-driven AI organizations in 2026.[^4][^5]

### Yes — Audacion AI Labs Can Be a PBC

California recognizes Benefit Corporations as a legal entity under the California Corporations Code. You can also incorporate as a **Delaware PBC** (the more VC-friendly and commonly recognized version) and qualify to do business in California. The two differ in reporting requirements:[^6][^7]
- **Delaware PBC:** Biennial mission impact report to shareholders; no public filing required; can raise VC/equity; not eligible for most government grants as a standalone entity[^8][^6]
- **California Benefit Corporation:** Annual benefit report posted publicly; must use third-party assessment standard[^7]

For Audacion AI Labs, **Delaware PBC incorporation is recommended** — it mirrors both Anthropic and OpenAI's structure exactly, is investor-friendly, allows Dee to retain full mission control, and signals serious intent to funders.[^3][^6]

### The Critical Limitation of a Pure PBC

A standalone PBC **cannot receive**:
- Federal government grants (NSF, NIH, DARPA) that require 501(c)(3) status or a government research entity[^9]
- Foundation grants that restrict to nonprofits (Open Philanthropy requires nonprofits or academic institutions, MacArthur requires nonprofits)[^10][^11]
- California state grants from programs tied to nonprofit status

This means a PBC alone locks Audacion AI Labs out of roughly 40-60% of the funding universe in this report. The solution is well-established in the AI safety field.

### Recommended Structure: Delaware PBC + Fiscal Sponsor

**The optimal structure for maximum funding access:**

| Entity | Purpose | Grant Access |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Audacion AI Labs, Inc.** (Delaware PBC) | The operating lab; the Anthropic-equivalent entity; raises impact investment, accepts corporate grants, holds IP | Corporate grants, CalOSBA Innovation, SBIR/STTR (small business track), VC/impact investment |
| **PRISM Research Institute** (fiscal sponsor 501(c)(3) OR standalone 501(c)(3)) | The grant-receiving entity; all federal and foundation grants flow here | NSF, NIH, DARPA, Open Philanthropy, MacArthur, Ford, Rockefeller, FLI, SFF |

**Two pathways to the nonprofit side:**

**Option A — Fiscal Sponsorship (Fastest, Lowest Cost):** A fiscal sponsor such as Rockefeller Philanthropy Advisors (which currently sponsors Humanity AI), Community Partners, or Social Good Fund acts as the 501(c)(3) umbrella. PRISM Research operates under their tax-exempt status and receives grants in their name. Typically costs 7-12% of grants received. This is the fastest path — can be operational in 30-60 days with no IRS application.[^12]

**Option B — Spin Up a 501(c)(3) (Most Grant Access, Most Work):** Incorporate PRISM Research Institute as a standalone 501(c)(3) public charity. This takes 6-12 months for IRS approval but maximizes independence and grant eligibility. California AB 1318 (signed October 2025) now protects state grant eligibility even if federal 501(c)(3) status is threatened.[^13]

**Recommended sequencing:** File for fiscal sponsorship now (30-60 days), begin applying for grants immediately, file for independent 501(c)(3) in parallel with the goal of achieving it within 12 months.
