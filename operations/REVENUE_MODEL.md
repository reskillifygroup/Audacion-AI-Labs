### Stream 2: Corporate Research Partnerships — $1M to $2M
Enterprise clients deploying AI agents subscribe to research access
Annual corporate membership: access to findings, drift analysis, governance frameworks
Not the raw dataset. The analyzed intelligence.
Target: 20 to 40 corporate partners at $25K to $50K annually

### Stream 3: Premium PRISM Research Reports — $500K to $1M
Free: blog posts, open research findings, community publications
Paid: quarterly PRISM reports with full data analysis, cross-model comparisons, governance recommendations
Pricing: $5K to $25K per report for enterprise buyers
Think Gartner or Forrester for AI safety
Target: 40 to 100 report sales per year

### Stream 4: Events and Conferences — $500K to $1M
Annual PRISM Summit: AI safety conference for researchers, practitioners, policymakers, contributors
Ticket sales plus sponsorships plus speaker fees
Quarterly corporate roundtables at premium pricing ($2K to $5K per seat)
Golf tournaments and networking events for funders and partners

### Stream 5: Training and Certification — $500K to $1M
PRISM Field Researcher certification: free at contributor level
Professional PRISM Certification for enterprise AI safety teams: paid
Companies send their people through training to learn post-deployment AI monitoring
Pricing: $2K to $5K per certification
Target: 200 to 500 certifications per year

### Stream 6: Consulting and Advisory — $500K to $1M
Enterprise AI deployment governance audits using the PRISM framework
Companies hire Audacion AI Labs to assess their AI systems
Leverages 30 years of workforce consulting experience applied to AI
Engagement pricing: $10K to $100K per audit depending on scope

### Stream 7: Merchandise and Community — $100K to $200K
PRISM branded merchandise

Brand building and culture revenue

Audacion AI Labs is not solely a grant-funded nonprofit research lab. It is a research institution that produces commercially valuable intelligence. The research is open. The premium analysis, training, certification, consulting, and reports are revenue. Grants fund the foundation. Revenue funds the growth.
