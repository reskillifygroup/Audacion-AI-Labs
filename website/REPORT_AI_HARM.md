## SECTION 1: OPENING

### If AI has caused harm to you or someone you know, we want to hear about it.

You do not need an account. You do not need to be a contributor. You do not need to explain yourself. Just tell us what happened.

Your report helps us understand what AI does in the real world so we can help make it safer for everyone.

## SECTION 2: CRISIS RESOURCES (always visible at top)

**If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please reach out now:**

**988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline** — Call or text 988. Available 24/7. Free and confidential.

**Crisis Text Line** — Text HOME to 741741. Available 24/7. Free and confidential.

**National Domestic Violence Hotline** — Call 1-800-799-7233 or text START to 88788.

**SAMHSA National Helpline** — Call 1-800-662-4357. Mental health and substance abuse referrals. Free, confidential, 24/7.

**International Association for Suicide Prevention** — https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/ (crisis centers by country for international visitors)

These resources are here because some AI harms touch on mental health, emotional wellbeing, and personal safety. You do not have to be in crisis to use this page. But if you are, please reach out to these services first. They are staffed by real people who can help right now.

## SECTION 3: HOW THIS WORKS

**Step 1:** You tell us what happened using the form below. No account needed. Anonymous reporting is available.

**Step 2:** Your report is reviewed by our Behavioral Health and Research team. If you selected "Urgent" or "Crisis," your report is prioritized for review within 24 hours.

**Step 3:** Based on your report and your consent choices, we may take one or more of the following actions:
- Provide you with additional resources and support
- Flag the behavior pattern for our research team to investigate
- Report the incident to the AI Incident Database (AIID) for public documentation
- Share the report with the AI platform involved so they can investigate and remediate

You control what happens with your report. Every action beyond our internal review requires your explicit consent.

## SECTION 5: WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU SUBMIT

Your report enters our review pipeline immediately.

**Informational and Concerning reports** are reviewed by our research team within one week. If your report reveals a pattern we are tracking or a new pattern we have not seen before, it contributes directly to our safety research.

**Urgent reports** are reviewed within 24 hours by our Behavioral Health Advisor. If you provided contact information and requested follow-up, we will reach out.

**Crisis reports** are reviewed within 24 hours and treated with the highest priority. If you indicated you or someone you know is in immediate danger and you provided contact information, we will reach out. If you did not provide contact information, your report still contributes to our understanding of how AI systems interact with people in crisis, and we use that understanding to push for safer systems.

Every report matters. Even if nothing further happens with yours individually, the pattern your report is part of shapes the research that makes AI safer for everyone who comes after you.

## SECTION 6: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Do I need an account to report?**
No. This form is available to anyone. You do not need to be a contributor, a researcher, or a member of any kind.

**Can I report anonymously?**
Yes. The contact information fields are optional. If you do not provide them, we cannot follow up with you, but your report still enters our system and contributes to our research (with your consent).

**What if I am not sure whether what happened counts as "harm"?**
Report it anyway. If something felt wrong, that matters. You do not need to be certain that what happened meets a legal or technical definition of harm. Your experience is valid and worth documenting.

**Will the AI company find out who I am?**
Only if you consent to it. The checkbox "share with the AI platform involved" is optional. If you do not check it, we do not share your report with the platform. If you do check it, we anonymize your personal information before sharing unless you explicitly tell us otherwise.

**What if the harm is ongoing and I need it to stop?**
If you are being harassed, stalked, or threatened through an AI platform, we encourage you to also report directly to that platform's trust and safety team and to local law enforcement if appropriate. We can help you understand your options if you request follow-up.

**I reported to the AI company and nothing happened. Can you help?**
We are a research lab, not a regulatory body. We cannot force a company to take action. But your report becomes part of a public research record that policymakers, journalists, and the companies themselves use to understand the scope of AI harms. Patterns documented in our data have a way of becoming impossible to ignore.

## DESIGN NOTES

- Background: can be dark like rest of site OR consider a slightly warmer/softer treatment for this page since the audience may be distressed. Test both.
- Crisis resources section: red left-border accent. Always visible. Does not collapse. Does not require scrolling past other content to find.
- Form: clean, spacious, not intimidating. Large text fields. Clear labels. No required fields that feel invasive.
- Urgency selector: if "Crisis" is selected, the crisis resources section should visually highlight (glow, expand, or animate to draw attention) BEFORE the rest of the form loads.
- Consent checkboxes: must be clearly worded. The required checkbox (internal review) should be visually distinct from the optional ones. No dark patterns. No pre-checked boxes.
- Submit button: prominent but not aggressive. Not red (red means danger on this page). Audacion purple.
- The overall tone of this page should feel safe. Not clinical. Not corporate. Not legal. A person who is hurting should feel like they found somewhere that takes them seriously.
- Mobile: form must work flawlessly on phone. Many people experiencing AI harm will report from their phone while it is happening.
- Accessibility: screen reader compatible. High contrast text. Clear tab order through form fields.
