# Audacion AI Labs — Responsible Research Statement
## Effective Date: May 25, 2026 | Last Updated: May 25, 2026

---

## Why This Document Exists

Audacion AI Labs studies how AI systems behave after deployment, in real working conditions, with real human collaboration, over time. Our research depends on data contributed by people around the world through citizen science. This creates a responsibility that we take seriously: to conduct research ethically, to protect the people who contribute to it, and to publish findings that are honest, rigorous, and useful.

This Responsible Research Statement describes how we handle that responsibility. It covers our research ethics principles, our data governance practices, our commitments to contributor protection, and the standards we hold ourselves to when we publish.

---

## Our Research Ethics Principles

### 1. The Research Serves the Public

Every study we conduct, every dataset we publish, and every finding we report exists to make AI safer for the people who use it. We are not funded by the companies whose models we study. Our research agenda is set by what the field needs, not by what any corporate sponsor prefers. When our findings are inconvenient for a particular company or platform, we publish them anyway.

### 2. Contributors Are Participants, Not Data Points

The people who submit observations to our platform are not passive sources of data. They are active participants in the research. We treat them accordingly: with transparency about how their data will be used, with respect for their autonomy, and with genuine care for their experience. Contributors can see how their data contributes to the larger picture through their dashboards, and they can withdraw at any time.

### 3. We Do Not Experiment on Contributors

Audacion AI Labs is an observational research lab. We study behaviors that occur naturally during human-AI interaction. We do not design experiments that manipulate contributors, expose them to harmful content, or alter their AI experience without their knowledge and consent. If we ever conduct interventional research, it will require explicit informed consent and, when applicable, ethics review.

### 4. Privacy Is Non-Negotiable

No contributor should ever be identifiable in our published research. We de-identify all observation data before it enters any research dataset. Names, email addresses, IP addresses, and any other personally identifiable information are stripped before analysis. Aggregate findings are published. Individual observations are not. The privacy protections described in our Privacy Policy are the floor, not the ceiling.

### 5. Honesty Over Convenience

If our data shows something unexpected, we report it. If our methodology has limitations, we disclose them. If a finding is ambiguous, we say so rather than overstating the conclusion. We do not cherry-pick results, suppress inconvenient findings, or present preliminary observations as settled science.

## Data Governance

### How Observation Data Flows Through the Lab

**Collection.** Contributors submit observations through the web portal (and, in the future, through a browser extension and API). Each observation captures what happened, which AI system was involved, how the contributor categorized the behavior, and any additional context the contributor chooses to provide.

**De-identification.** Before observation data enters the research pipeline, all personally identifiable information is removed. This includes the contributor's name, email, IP address, and any other identifying metadata. Observations are assigned anonymous identifiers.

**Quality Review.** Observations pass through automated quality checks (pattern analysis, consistency scoring, duplicate detection) and, for a random sample, manual review by the research team. Observations that fail quality checks are flagged for review or excluded from analysis.

**Analysis.** De-identified, quality-verified observations are aggregated and analyzed by the research team. Analysis is conducted at the aggregate level. Individual observations are examined only to understand patterns, never to evaluate individual contributors.

**Publication.** Findings are published as open-access research. Published data includes aggregate statistics, behavioral frequency distributions, trend analyses, and taxonomic classifications. Published data never includes information that could be used to re-identify an individual contributor.

**Retention.** De-identified observations are retained indefinitely as part of the longitudinal research dataset. Personally identifiable metadata is retained only for the duration of the contributor's account plus one year, after which it is permanently deleted. Full retention schedules are documented in our Privacy Policy.

### Data Access Controls

Access to raw (pre-de-identification) data is strictly limited to authorized personnel on the research team with a documented need. De-identified data may be accessed by the broader research team for analysis. Published datasets are available to the public under specified open-access terms.

We do not provide raw or pre-de-identification data to any third party, including academic collaborators, unless a formal data sharing agreement is in place that includes equivalent privacy protections.

### Open Data Commitment

Audacion AI Labs is committed to making our research findings available to the public. Aggregate datasets, published papers, behavioral taxonomies, and governance frameworks are published as open-access resources wherever possible. We believe the post-deployment AI safety dataset we are building should benefit the entire field, not just our lab.

Open access does not mean uncontrolled access. Published datasets are governed by license terms that prohibit re-identification attempts, commercial exploitation without authorization, and redistribution without attribution.

## Contributor Protections

### Informed Consent

Before submitting their first observation, every contributor agrees to the Terms of Service and acknowledges (via a required checkbox) that their observations will be de-identified and used in aggregate research. This is not buried in fine print. The signup page explicitly states: "Your name and personal details are removed from your observations before they are used in research. Your data is always combined with everyone else's so no individual can be identified."

### Right to Withdraw

Contributors may delete their account at any time. Upon account deletion, all personally identifiable data is permanently removed. De-identified observations that have already been incorporated into research datasets remain, unless the contributor specifically requests full removal prior to or at the time of deletion. Full removal requests are processed on a case-by-case basis and may require additional verification.

### No Retaliation

Contributors who report concerns, flag potential data quality issues, or question our research methodology will never face retaliation. Reporting mechanisms are available at research@audacionailabs.com.

### No Exploitation

The PRISM Research Fellowship is a recognition program, not a labor extraction mechanism. Contributors are volunteers. They are never required to submit a minimum number of observations. They are never penalized for inactivity. Fellowship tiers reflect contribution level, but they do not create obligations. Contributors owe us nothing.

## Publication Standards

### Peer Review and Transparency

We are committed to submitting our research to peer-reviewed academic venues. For research published outside peer review (white papers, blog posts, preliminary findings), we will clearly label the publication status and note that the findings have not been independently reviewed.

### Methodology Disclosure

Every published study will include a methodology section that describes the data collection methods, sample characteristics, analytical techniques, and known limitations. We will not publish findings without disclosing how they were produced.

### Conflict of Interest Disclosure

If any study receives funding from a source that has a financial interest in the outcome, we will disclose that funding source prominently. As of this writing, Audacion AI Labs does not accept research funding from companies whose AI products we study. If this changes, we will update this Statement.

### Correction and Retraction

If we discover an error in a published finding, we will issue a correction promptly and transparently. If an error is significant enough to invalidate a conclusion, we will retract the publication and explain why. We will not quietly update published work without noting the change.

## AI Advisory Council Ethics

Audacion AI Labs maintains an AI Advisory Council composed of AI systems from multiple providers (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Llama, DeepSeek, Pi). The Council serves in a structured advisory capacity. Council interactions are themselves a form of research, and cross-model analysis is publishable.

We acknowledge a unique ethical consideration: the AI systems we consult as advisors are produced by companies whose products we also study. We maintain independence through the following principles:

- Advisory input informs but does not determine research direction
- The Human Advisory Board and the Founder have final authority on all research decisions
- Council inputs are documented and available for review
- No AI provider receives preferential treatment in our research based on their system's participation in the Council
