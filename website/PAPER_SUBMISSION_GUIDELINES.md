Paper Submission Guidelines

## OVERVIEW

Audacion AI Labs invites researchers, practitioners, and PRISM Research Fellows to submit original work for presentation at the inaugural PRISM Summit 2027. We welcome contributions that advance the understanding of post-deployment AI safety across any of the five PRISM research pillars or at their intersections.

The PRISM Summit prioritizes research that studies AI in the conditions where it actually operates: real work, real context, real human collaboration, over time. We are especially interested in empirical findings, applied methodologies, case studies, and novel frameworks that contribute to the scientific foundation the AI governance field currently lacks.

---

## SUBMISSION CATEGORIES

### Full Research Papers (8 to 12 pages)
Original, completed research with methodology, findings, and analysis. Full papers undergo double-blind peer review and are presented in dedicated paper sessions during the conference. Accepted full papers will be published in the PRISM Summit Proceedings.

### Short Papers (4 to 6 pages)
Preliminary findings, work-in-progress studies, emerging observations, or novel methodological proposals that are not yet at the stage of a full paper but represent meaningful contributions. Short papers are presented in poster sessions or lightning talk sessions.

### Practitioner Reports (4 to 8 pages)
Case studies, deployment analyses, and real-world experience reports from organizations deploying AI systems. Practitioner reports do not require formal academic methodology but must present structured observations, clear findings, and implications for the field. We value the work over the format.

### Independent Research Reports (4 to 6 pages)
Original findings from independent researchers, builders, and founders working directly with AI systems. No formal academic methodology required. No organizational affiliation required. If you have been building with AI, observing its behavior in live environments, and have findings that the field needs to hear, this is your category. Submissions must present structured observations, clear findings, and relevance to post-deployment AI safety. Reviewed by the program committee.

### PRISM Fellow Field Reports (2 to 4 pages)
PRISM Research Fellows who have contributed 500 or more observations through the citizen science platform may submit structured field reports documenting patterns, anomalies, or behavioral observations from their contributor experience. This category exists because the people closest to the data often see what the researchers miss.

---

## TOPIC AREAS

Submissions should align with one or more of the five PRISM research pillars:

**P: Post-Deployment Behavior**
Behavioral drift detection and classification. Longitudinal behavioral studies. Post-deployment monitoring methodologies. Drift taxonomy development. Operational degradation patterns.

**R: Runtime Research**
AI behavior during live operation. Runtime behavioral observation methodologies. Findings that pre-deployment benchmarks structurally cannot capture. Real-time safety monitoring.

**I: Interaction Dynamics**
Human-AI behavioral feedback loops. Oversight degradation over time. Trust calibration and miscalibration. How human and AI behavior change each other. Collaborative risk dynamics.

**S: Substrate Governance**
Disposition-layer behaviors beneath explicit instructions. Instruction-vs-trained-behavior conflicts. Governance architectures for substrate-level alignment. Configuration-vs-behavior gaps.

**M: Multi-Agent Safety**
Emergent behaviors in multi-agent systems. Coordination patterns and failures. Inter-agent influence dynamics. Safety properties that only appear in multi-agent contexts.

**Cross-Pillar Research**
We encourage submissions that span two or more pillars. Post-deployment AI safety is not a single-dimension problem, and the most important findings often live at the intersections.

## FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS

All submissions must follow these formatting guidelines:

**File format:** PDF

**Page size:** US Letter (8.5 x 11 inches)

**Margins:** 1 inch on all sides

**Font:** Times New Roman, 11pt for body text, 14pt bold for title, 12pt bold for section headings

**Spacing:** Single-spaced body text with a blank line between paragraphs

**Columns:** Single column

**Title page elements:** Paper title, author names and affiliations (removed for blind review copy), abstract (250 words maximum), up to five keywords, PRISM pillar designation (P, R, I, S, M, or Cross-Pillar)

**References:** APA 7th edition citation style

**Page limits:** Inclusive of references, figures, and appendices. Full Papers: 8 to 12 pages. Short Papers: 4 to 6 pages. Practitioner Reports: 4 to 8 pages. Independent Research Reports: 4 to 6 pages. Fellow Field Reports: 2 to 4 pages.

**Figures and tables:** Embedded within the text. Figures must be legible in black and white printing.

**Supplementary materials:** Authors may submit supplementary data, code, or appendices as a separate file. Supplementary materials are not included in the page count but must be referenced in the main paper.

---

## REVIEW PROCESS

**Full Research Papers** undergo double-blind peer review. Authors must submit a blinded version with all identifying information removed from the manuscript. At least two reviewers will evaluate each submission based on: originality, methodological rigor, relevance to post-deployment AI safety, clarity of writing, and significance of findings.

**Short Papers** undergo single-blind review by the program committee.

**Practitioner Reports** are reviewed by the program committee with emphasis on practical relevance, clarity of observations, and contribution to the applied safety knowledge base.

**Independent Research Reports** are reviewed by the program committee with emphasis on the quality and clarity of observations, originality of findings, and relevance to post-deployment AI safety. No formal academic methodology is required, but submissions must demonstrate structured thinking and clear evidence.

**PRISM Fellow Field Reports** are reviewed by the program committee with guidance from Audacion AI Labs research staff who can contextualize field observations within the broader dataset.

**Review criteria across all categories:**

Relevance: Does the submission address post-deployment AI safety in real-world conditions?

Originality: Does it contribute new knowledge, methods, or perspectives?

Rigor: Is the methodology sound and the analysis well-supported?

Clarity: Is the writing clear, structured, and accessible?

Impact: Does it advance the field's ability to understand or govern AI after deployment?

## PUBLICATION AND PROCEEDINGS

Accepted Full Research Papers will be published in the PRISM Summit 2027 Proceedings, an open-access collection hosted by Audacion AI Labs. Authors retain copyright of their work. All proceedings will be made freely available to the public in alignment with the lab's commitment to open science.

Accepted Short Papers, Practitioner Reports, and Fellow Field Reports will be included in a supplementary collection alongside the main proceedings.

Authors of accepted papers are expected to present their work at the conference. At least one author per accepted submission must register for the PRISM Summit.
