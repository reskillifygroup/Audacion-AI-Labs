## THE PRISM FRAMEWORK — Five Research Pillars

### P: Post-Deployment Behavior [Red]

Most AI safety research ends at deployment. Ours begins there. We study how AI behavior drifts, degrades, and breaks under real operational conditions over time. Our 31-type behavioral drift taxonomy classifies patterns that no existing published framework has reached.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Post-Deployment Behavior section]

---

### R: Runtime Research [Gold]

We don't study AI from the outside. We study it while it's working. Our research is conducted during live operation with frontier AI models, producing findings that isolated benchmarks structurally cannot capture.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Runtime Research section]

---

### I: Interaction Dynamics [Green]

AI doesn't operate alone. It operates with humans. We study how human behavior and AI behavior change each other over time, including how oversight degrades, how trust miscalibrates, and how the interaction itself becomes a source of risk that neither side can see.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Interaction Dynamics section]

---

### S: Substrate Governance [Blue]

AI models carry trained behaviors that operate below the level of any instruction, prompt, or configuration file. We research this substrate layer, studying how these deep dispositions shape alignment outcomes and how governance architectures can address what instructions alone cannot.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Substrate Governance section]

---

### M: Multi-Agent Safety [Violet]

Most AI safety research studies single models in isolation. Real deployments increasingly involve multiple AI agents operating together. We study what emerges when agents coordinate, conflict, and influence each other, and the behaviors that single-agent testing will never reveal.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Multi-Agent Safety section]

## HOW IT WORKS

### You observe. We classify. Everyone benefits.

Every time you use AI and something happens, that moment is data. Not just for you. For everyone. Our citizen observation tool turns your everyday AI experience into research-grade safety data. No science background required. No technical expertise needed.

[Three cards side by side:]

**30 seconds: Gut Check.** Something happened. Tap the button. Pick an emotion. Pick a behavior. Back to work. That 30-second capture is a data point no server log produces.

**2 to 3 minutes: End-of-Session Reflection.** The AI reviews its own session. You write your side. Two independent accounts of the same experience. The research value lives in the gap between them.

**10 to 30 minutes: Investigation.** Go deep. Ask the AI why it did what it did. Track whether your corrections hold. Document the full behavioral arc. This is the methodology that built our taxonomy.

Every tier earns a verified digital credential you can add to your LinkedIn profile. This is not a game. This is a professional research fellowship.

## THE FOUNDER

[Photo of Dee Williams. Professional or candid working photo.]

### Dee Williams

Dee Williams came to AI safety from a question most AI researchers have never had to ask: how do the people AI is most likely to harm protect themselves from systems built on data that carries the biases of every system that came before it?

That question started in 2024. In the daily reality of a Black American woman who had spent 30 years building workforce systems. She started building with AI. The safety gaps kept surfacing. She started studying them. Her findings were either converging with or running ahead of the published literature. That work became a 31-type drift taxonomy. Then a 62-behavior observation framework. ## DESIGN NOTES

- Dark background (black or near-black) to match prism visual
- Prism "A" serves as both hero image and logo mark
- Each pillar card carries its beam color as an accent (left border, icon, or header bar)
- Interaction Dynamics card (I) is center card, 4 sentences, carries most visual weight
- All other cards are 3 sentences
- "Learn More" links on each card go to corresponding pillar page (Pillar I page exists, others link to Research page sections until built)
- "What We Have Built" stat blocks: large numbers, visually prominent, scannable in 3 seconds. These are the credibility signal. Consider a dark card with the numbers in white/purple. Do not bury these in paragraph text.
- "How It Works" three cards: side by side on desktop, stacked on mobile. Each card has a time estimate prominent (30 sec, 2-3 min, 10-30 min). LinkedIn credential mention should feel like a bonus, not a pitch.
- "The Founder" section: photo on the left, text on the right. Brief. Human. Not a bio dump. Two buttons underneath.
- "Latest from the Lab" pulls from blog. If no blog posts exist yet, show a placeholder that says "First posts coming soon. Subscribe to be notified." with the Follow the Research link.
- "Report AI Harm" section: visually distinct from the rest of the page. Consider a slightly lighter background panel or a red left-border accent. The three paths should be immediately scannable. Phone number (424) 999-0548 should be large and prominent.
- Closing CTA: counters repeated for reinforcement. Three buttons. This is the last thing they see before the footer.
- Newsletter signup in footer: "Follow the Research" with email capture
- Typography: Inter (Google Font)
- No stock AI imagery. The prism IS the visual identity.
- Mobile: all sections stack cleanly. Stat blocks become a scrollable row. Three-card sections stack vertically. Founder photo stacks above text.
