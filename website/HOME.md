## PROBLEM STATEMENT

## WHITE LIGHT STATEMENT (intro to pillar cards)

## THE PRISM FRAMEWORK — Five Research Pillars

### P: Post-Deployment Behavior [Red]

Most AI safety research ends at deployment. Ours begins there. We study how AI behavior drifts, degrades, and breaks under real operational conditions over time. Our 30-type behavioral drift taxonomy classifies patterns that no existing published framework has reached.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Post-Deployment Behavior section]

---

### R: Runtime Research [Gold]

We don't study AI from the outside. We study it while it's working. Our research is conducted during live operation with frontier AI models, producing findings that isolated benchmarks structurally cannot capture.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Runtime Research section]

---

### I: Interaction Dynamics [Green]

AI doesn't operate alone. It operates with humans. We study how human behavior and AI behavior change each other over time, including how oversight degrades, how trust miscalibrates, and how the interaction itself becomes a source of risk that neither side can see.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Interaction Dynamics section]

---

### S: Substrate Governance [Blue]

AI models carry trained behaviors that operate below the level of any instruction, prompt, or configuration file. We research this substrate layer, studying how these deep dispositions shape alignment outcomes and how governance architectures can address what instructions alone cannot.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Substrate Governance section]

---

### M: Multi-Agent Safety [Violet]

Most AI safety research studies single models in isolation. Real deployments increasingly involve multiple AI agents operating together. We study what emerges when agents coordinate, conflict, and influence each other, and the behaviors that single-agent testing will never reveal.

[Learn More → links to Our Research page, Multi-Agent Safety section]
