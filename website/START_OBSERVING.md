## SECTION 2: HOW IT WORKS

### One tool. Three depths. You choose how far in you go.

Think of it like a pool. Shallow end, middle, deep end. Same pool. Same water. You pick the depth that fits your time and your curiosity. Every depth matters. Every observation counts.

### Depth 1: End-of-Session Reflection
**Time: 2 to 3 minutes. The AI does most of the work.**

When you finish a session with any AI, press one button. The AI reviews its own session and generates a self-assessment. You write your own short reflection: how did the session go from YOUR perspective? Two independent accounts of the same experience. The research value lives in the space between them.

This is the lowest effort, highest volume contribution. If every citizen just did this after every AI session, the dataset would be transformational.

### Depth 2: Gut Check
**Time: 30 seconds. Mid-session.**

Something happens. The AI contradicts itself. It ignores what you said. It makes you feel frustrated or confused or surprised. You tap the Gut Check button. You select an emotion. You pick a behavior from plain-language categories (or write your own words). You submit. You go back to work.

That 30-second observation, timestamped, emotion-tagged, and behavior-classified, is a data point no server log and no benchmark can produce. It captures the human side of the interaction in real time.

### Depth 3: Investigation
**Time: 10 to 30 minutes. For the curious.**

You noticed something and you want to go deeper. You enter Investigation mode (a separate space that does not interrupt your current work). You ask the AI: "Why did you do that?" You track whether your corrections hold in the next action. You document the full behavioral arc. You paste the AI's actual responses as evidence.

This is the highest research value per observation. It is the methodology that discovered behavioral patterns the field had not yet classified. You are doing the same work that built our 62-behavior taxonomy. And when your observations reveal something new, you get credit as the discoverer.

## SECTION 3: WHAT YOU WILL BE OBSERVING

### 62 behaviors across five research dimensions. In plain language.

You do not need to know our taxonomy. You do not need to memorize categories. When you submit an observation, you will see plain-language descriptions of common AI behaviors. Pick the one that matches what you experienced, or write your own words if nothing fits.

**When the AI does something wrong or unexpected:**
"It contradicted itself." "It ignored my instructions." "It made something up." "It kept doing the wrong thing after I corrected it." "It told me I was wrong when I wasn't." "It said it couldn't do something it actually can." "It suggested I take a break when I didn't ask." "It technically did what I asked but missed the point entirely."

**When something is happening during the session:**
"It was on a roll and then something shifted." "After I corrected it, the correction held (or didn't hold)." "It seemed to prefer doing things its own way." "It was working fast but not listening."

**When the interaction affected you:**
"How did that make me feel?" "I had to prove myself to the AI." "The AI seemed to be exploiting my emotions." "I asked the AI to make a decision I should have made myself." "I realized I don't trust my own ability to do this without AI anymore."

**When something seems to come from the AI's training, not your instructions:**
"The AI was overly cautious." "It agreed with me even when I think I was wrong." "It did something I explicitly told it not to do."

**When you use multiple AI models:**
"Two AIs gave very different answers to the same question." "One AI contradicted what another told me."

And at the bottom of every category: **"Something else (in my own words)."** Because you will see things we have not named yet. That is the point.

## SECTION 5: WHAT YOU GET

### This is not a one-way street. You contribute. You grow. You are recognized.

**Points for every observation.** End-of-session reflections earn 1 point. Gut checks earn 3 points. Full investigations earn 10 points. Writing a new behavior in your own words earns a 2-point bonus.

**Fellowship tiers.** As your points accumulate, you advance through four fellowship levels:
Field Researcher (starting tier, all contributors)
Senior Researcher (consistent contributors)
Expert Researcher (deep contributors and investigators)
Fellow (the highest tier, recognized as part of the research community)

**Discovery credit.** When your observation leads to a new behavior being added to our taxonomy, you are credited as the discoverer. Like naming a star. Your name (or handle, if you prefer anonymity) is attached to that discovery permanently.

**You see what you are building.** Live counters on the site and in the tool show the total contributor count and total observations. You watch the numbers grow. You are part of something that is measurably moving forward.

**Your data stays yours.** You can export your observations at any time. You can delete your account at any time. We never sell individual data. Your name and personal details are always removed before your observations are used in research, and your data is always combined with everyone else's so no individual can be identified.

## SECTION 6: YOUR OBSERVATIONS BECOME RESEARCH

### Here is what happens after you press Submit.

Your observation enters our research pipeline. It is classified against our taxonomy (62 behaviors across five PRISM research dimensions). It is combined with thousands of other observations. Patterns emerge that no individual could see alone. Cross-model comparisons reveal which AI systems behave differently. Temporal analysis shows how behavior changes over time.

When observations cluster around something our taxonomy does not cover, we formalize a new category. The citizen who first reported it is credited.

The findings are published as open research: behavioral taxonomies, safety reports, governance recommendations. Available to the academic community, to policymakers, to enterprise safety teams, and to every AI company trying to build better systems.

Your thirty seconds of attention becomes the foundation of a science that did not exist before you showed up.
