## WHAT YOUR FUNDING ENABLES

Every dollar that enters Audacion AI Labs goes directly toward building the scientific foundation that AI governance currently lacks.

### The Research
Fund the studies that produce the missing science. Your funding builds the field's first post-deployment AI behavior taxonomy: 62 observable behaviors mapped across five PRISM dimensions, 15 of which exist in no published framework anywhere. It produces longitudinal studies tracking how AI systems behave over time in real-world conditions, published findings the entire field can build on, and open frameworks that become the foundation for AI governance policy. Less than 2% of AI safety research covers this territory. Your grant changes that number.

### The Tool
Fund the citizen science platform that makes one billion observations possible. A browser extension that lets any AI user report a behavioral observation in 30 seconds. A reporting interface with three engagement depths. A five-layer data pipeline that transforms a single citizen observation into classified, validated, publishable research data. An API layer that lets researchers query the world's first post-deployment AI safety dataset. This is the infrastructure. Your funding builds it.

### The People
Fund the researchers, fellows, and staff who turn raw observations into real science and real solutions. Analysts who find the patterns. Researchers who publish the findings. Engineers who build the tools. A dedicated Behavioral Health Advisor who reviews harm reports from citizens experiencing real damage from AI systems. Policy minds who translate the science into governance that organizations and governments can actually use. Every hire accelerates the path from a field observation to a safer AI system.

### The Community
Fund the community infrastructure that connects one million contributors to each other and to the lab. The PRISM Research Fellowship that takes citizens from first observation to full fellowship status. The citizen discovery attribution program that credits contributors who identify new AI behaviors, like astronomers discovering new objects. Events. Recognition. A movement requires more than a mission. It requires a home.

## WHY THIS LAB

There are hundreds of AI safety organizations. Here is why this one is different.

We don't study AI in isolation. We study it where it works, where it breaks, and where it interacts with humans over time. No other lab occupies this position.

We don't keep the research behind closed doors. Our findings are open. Our dataset is governed. The science we produce belongs to the field.

We don't recruit from one discipline. Our contributors span every industry, every country, and every level of AI experience. The dataset we build is the most diverse post-deployment behavioral dataset in existence because the people who build it are.

And we don't wait for a crisis to study safety. We build the science before the governance needs it, so that when policy catches up, there is something real underneath it.

Our AI Advisory Council puts the same research questions to seven frontier AI models (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Llama, DeepSeek, Pi). Cross-model analysis is itself publishable research. No other lab has this governance structure.

## WAYS TO SUPPORT

### Grant Funding
Fund specific research projects, tool development, or fellowship programs. We are actively applying for NSF SBIR, Cartier Women's Initiative, CalOSBA Innovation Grants, and other aligned funding programs. Your grant accelerates our timeline. We welcome conversations about directed grants toward specific PRISM pillars, the citizen science platform, or the harm response system.

### Institutional Partnership
Partner with us as a university research collaborator, a corporate research sponsor, or an institutional ally. We welcome partnerships that expand the lab's reach, including partnerships with organizations whose models we study. Our editorial independence is structural: partners do not see findings before publication, partners do not approve what we publish, and collaborative access does not come with editorial influence. Every partnership and funding relationship is publicly disclosed. That boundary is not a policy. It is architecture.

### Sponsorship
Sponsor the PRISM Research Fellowship program, the annual PRISM Summit (inaugural September 2027, Los Angeles), community events, or specific research publications. Your brand aligns with the future of AI safety.

### Data Partnership
Contribute model access, computational resources, or anonymized datasets that expand the lab's research capacity. If your organization deploys AI systems and wants to understand how they behave in post-deployment conditions, our citizen science data and research methodology can provide insights no internal telemetry can capture.

### Advisory
Join our advisory network. If you bring expertise in AI safety, research methodology, grant strategy, policy, community building, or citizen science, your guidance is as valuable as funding.
