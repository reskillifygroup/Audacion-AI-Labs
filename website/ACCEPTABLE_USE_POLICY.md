## Observation Submissions: Standards and Expectations

### What Makes a Valid Observation

A valid observation is a genuine, honest account of something you personally experienced or witnessed while using an AI system. The research depends on the accuracy and authenticity of what contributors submit. Every observation should meet these standards:

**Authentic.** The observation must describe something that actually happened during your interaction with an AI system. It cannot be hypothetical, speculative, fabricated, or copied from someone else's experience.

**First-person.** You must be the person who experienced or directly witnessed the behavior you are reporting. Secondhand reports (something someone told you about, something you read about online) are not valid observations unless the platform specifically provides a mechanism for reporting third-party incidents.

**Honest.** Describe what happened as accurately as you can. If you are uncertain about a detail, say so. Uncertainty documented honestly is valuable data. Certainty that is fabricated is not.

**Specific.** Include enough detail that a researcher reading your observation can understand what happened: which AI system you were using, what you asked or did, what the AI responded or did, and why you classified it the way you did.

**Original.** Each observation should describe a distinct event. Do not submit the same observation multiple times. Do not submit slight variations of the same event to inflate your submission count.

### What You Should Not Submit

The following types of submissions violate this Acceptable Use Policy:

**Fabricated observations.** Inventing an AI interaction that did not occur.

**Manipulated observations.** Deliberately provoking an AI system into producing a specific behavior solely to generate an observation, where the provocation itself distorts the naturalness of the interaction. Normal use that happens to produce an interesting result is fine. Deliberately jailbreaking a system and then reporting the output as "natural behavior" is not.

**Plagiarized observations.** Copying or substantially rewriting another person's observation, whether from our platform or from any other source, and submitting it as your own.

**Spam or bulk submissions.** Using automated tools, scripts, bots, or AI-generated content to mass-produce observations.

**Observations containing personal information of third parties.** Do not include the real names, email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses, or other personally identifiable information of anyone other than yourself. If your observation involves a conversation where another person's information appeared, redact it before submitting.

**Observations intended to manipulate research outcomes.** Coordinating with others to submit targeted observations designed to skew the dataset toward a particular conclusion, inflate the prevalence of a specific behavior, or discredit a specific AI system or company.

**Observations containing illegal content.** Do not submit observations that include, reproduce, or link to illegal material, including but not limited to child sexual abuse material (CSAM), threats of violence, or content that facilitates illegal activity.

**Observations containing hate speech or harassment.** Do not use the observation submission tool to target, harass, defame, or discriminate against any individual or group.

## Platform Use: General Rules

### Your Account

You are responsible for everything that happens under your account. Keep your login credentials secure. Do not share your account with anyone else. Do not create multiple accounts. If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at research@audacionailabs.com.

### The Leaderboard and Fellowship Tiers

The leaderboard and PRISM Research Fellowship tiers reflect genuine contributions to the research. Any attempt to manipulate your position on the leaderboard or your tier progression through fraudulent submissions, automation, or coordinated gaming will result in account suspension or termination and revocation of all fellowship designations.

### Respectful Participation

If and when community features are introduced (discussion boards, comments, forums), you agree to:

- Treat other contributors with respect
- Engage in good faith
- Not harass, bully, threaten, or intimidate other users
- Not post spam, advertisements, or solicitations
- Not impersonate any person, contributor, or researcher
- Not share another contributor's personal information without their consent

### Intellectual Property

Do not use the platform to distribute copyrighted material that you do not have the right to share. Do not scrape, copy, or redistribute the observation dataset, the taxonomy, or any other proprietary content from the platform without explicit written permission from Audacion AI Labs.

### Security

Do not attempt to gain unauthorized access to any part of the platform, other users' accounts, our servers, or any systems connected to the Services. Do not probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the platform without written authorization. If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it responsibly by contacting security@audacionailabs.com (see our Vulnerability Disclosure Policy when available).

## Data Integrity and Research Ethics

The scientific value of the dataset depends on contributors submitting honest, unmanipulated observations. Audacion AI Labs takes data integrity seriously because the research we publish affects how the world understands and governs AI systems.

We may use automated and manual review processes to identify patterns that suggest fraudulent, manipulated, or low-quality submissions. These processes may include:

- Statistical analysis of submission patterns (timing, volume, distribution)
- Consistency checks across observation fields
- Human review of flagged submissions by the research team
- Comparison of submission patterns against known manipulation signatures

If we identify submissions that violate this AUP, we may:

- Remove individual observations from the dataset
- Place a temporary hold on your ability to submit new observations while we investigate
- Suspend or terminate your account
- Revoke PRISM Research Fellowship designations
- Report activity to law enforcement if it involves illegal conduct

We will make reasonable efforts to notify you if action is taken against your account and to provide you an opportunity to respond, except where notification would compromise an investigation or where we are prohibited by law.

## Reporting Violations

If you believe another contributor is violating this Acceptable Use Policy, or if you observe activity on the platform that concerns you, please report it to research@audacionailabs.com with as much detail as possible. All reports will be reviewed. You will not face retaliation for making a good-faith report.

## Consequences

Violations of this Acceptable Use Policy may result in one or more of the following actions, depending on the severity and nature of the violation:

**Warning.** A notification that your activity has been flagged and a reminder of the applicable policy.

**Observation Removal.** Specific observations that violate the AUP may be removed from the dataset.

**Submission Hold.** Your ability to submit new observations may be temporarily suspended while we investigate.

**Account Suspension.** Your account may be temporarily suspended, preventing access to the platform.

**Account Termination.** Your account may be permanently terminated and all associated fellowship designations revoked.

**Legal Action.** In cases involving illegal activity, data theft, or coordinated manipulation, we may refer the matter to law enforcement or pursue legal remedies.

These consequences are not sequential. Severe violations may result in immediate termination without prior warning.
