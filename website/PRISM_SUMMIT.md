## ABOUT

The PRISM Summit is a three-day conference hosted by Audacion AI Labs bringing together leading researchers, practitioners, industry partners, and PRISM Research Fellows to showcase the latest developments in post-deployment AI safety research and foster collaboration within our growing community.

The AI safety field has spent years studying models in isolation. The PRISM Summit is where the field meets the real world. Over three days, attendees will engage with original research across all five PRISM pillars, hear from researchers and builders working at the frontier of applied AI safety, and connect with a community that believes safe AI requires more than theory.

This is not a conference about what might go wrong. This is a conference about what we are building to make it go right.

**What to expect:**

Research presentations and paper sessions across the five PRISM pillars: Post-Deployment Behavior, Runtime Research, Interaction Dynamics, Substrate Governance, and Multi-Agent Safety.

Keynote addresses from researchers, founders, and practitioners shaping the future of AI safety.

Industry partner showcases and sponsor activations.

Workshops and working sessions designed for hands-on collaboration.

Networking events, community gatherings, and experiences that go beyond the main stage.

A dedicated track for PRISM Research Fellows presenting citizen science findings from the field.

**Our goal for the inaugural summit is 300 attendees.** Researchers, practitioners, policymakers, enterprise AI teams, PRISM Research Fellows, students, and anyone committed to making AI safer in the real world.

## CALL FOR PAPERS

Audacion AI Labs invites original research contributions for presentation at the PRISM Summit 2027. We welcome submissions across all five PRISM research pillars:

**P: Post-Deployment Behavior**
Research on AI behavioral drift, degradation, and breakdown under real operational conditions. Drift taxonomies, longitudinal behavioral studies, and post-deployment monitoring methodologies.

**R: Runtime Research**
Research conducted during live AI operation. Findings that isolated benchmarks and pre-deployment testing structurally cannot capture.

**I: Interaction Dynamics**
Research on human-AI behavioral feedback loops, oversight degradation, trust calibration, and how human behavior and AI behavior change each other over time.

**S: Substrate Governance**
Research on the disposition layer beneath explicit instructions. How trained behaviors shape alignment outcomes and how governance architectures address what instructions alone cannot.

**M: Multi-Agent Safety**
Research on emergent behaviors, coordination patterns, and safety dynamics when multiple AI agents operate together.

**Cross-Pillar Submissions**
We also welcome research that spans two or more pillars. Post-deployment AI safety does not live in silos, and neither should the research.

**Who should submit:**
Academic researchers, independent researchers, builders and founders working directly with AI systems, industry practitioners, PRISM Research Fellows with substantial field findings, and interdisciplinary teams. We accept five submission types: Full Research Papers, Short Papers, Practitioner Reports, Independent Research Reports, and PRISM Fellow Field Reports. We value the work over the credential. If your research contributes to post-deployment AI safety, we want to see it.
