## HEADLINE

Research in real time.

---

## PAGE DESCRIPTION

In the beginning, this blog carries the observations of our founder. As the PRISM Research Fellowship grows, it will carry the findings of one million contributors across five research pillars. Every post represents what the field learns when AI safety research moves from isolation to reality.

---

## BLOG CATEGORIES

### P: Post-Deployment Behavior [Red]
Blog posts about drift observations, behavioral degradation, what happens after AI ships.

### R: Runtime Research [Gold]
Blog posts about what we observe while working with AI in real time. The practitioner lens.

### I: Interaction Dynamics [Green]
Blog posts about human-AI behavioral loops, oversight degradation, how humans and AI change each other.

### S: Substrate Governance [Blue]
Blog posts about the disposition layer, instruction vs trained behavior, governance architecture.

### M: Multi-Agent Safety [Violet]
Blog posts about what happens when multiple AI agents operate together, emergent behaviors, coordination patterns.

### From the Lab [White]
Lab announcements, milestones, partnership news, fellowship updates, and operational updates that are not research findings.

---

## SOURCE TAGS (applied to each research post)

Founder Observation: Finding comes from Dee Williams' direct work with AI.
Community Finding: Finding comes from PRISM Field Researcher contributor data.
Cross-Model Study: Finding comes from AI Advisory Council cross-model analysis.

## FIRST FIVE POSTS (launch roadmap, in order)

### Post 1: Why Claude Keeps Telling You to Sleep (And Why Your Fix Doesn't Work)
Category: S (Substrate Governance) [Blue]
Source tag: Founder Observation
Summary: The Fortune article and Reddit thread are trending. This post explains the real reason: the instruction layer vs the disposition layer. Introduces the concept that configuration files are not behavior. Demonstrates structural thinking. Does not reveal any internal architecture or proprietary frameworks. Establishes Dee as someone who understands this problem at a depth the field is visibly fumbling.
Timing: Publish week 1. Ride the wave while the story is still fresh.

### Post 2: The PRISM Shift: Why We Started a Research Lab
Category: From the Lab [White]
Summary: The origin story of Audacion AI Labs written as a blog post. Introduces the PRISM framework, the five research pillars, the citizen science model, and the impact goal. This is the post you share on LinkedIn the day the site goes live. It's the lab's public debut.
Timing: Publish day 1 of site launch.

### Post 3: We Found 30 Types of AI Drift. The Field Has Published 3.
Category: P (Post-Deployment Behavior) [Red]
Source tag: Founder Observation
Summary: References the drift taxonomy survey showing 26 published frameworks. Positions Audacion AI Labs' 30-type taxonomy against the most comprehensive published work (3 types). Does not publish the full taxonomy. Establishes Dee as the leading voice in drift research.
Timing: Publish week 2 or 3. This is the credibility post.

### Post 4: What The AI Doc Got Right and What It Missed
Category: R (Runtime Research) [Gold]
Source tag: Founder Observation
Summary: Dee's review of the Roher documentary from a builder's perspective. References Tristan Harris, Marvin Harris and Cultural Materialism, the Anthropic blackmail study, and the gap between what the documentary worries about and what a builder is already solving architecturally. Positions the lab as the answer to questions the film could not answer.
Timing: Publish week 3 or 4.

### Post 5: Citizen Science for AI Safety: Why We Need One Million Observers
Category: From the Lab [White]
Summary: Introduces the PRISM Research Fellowship publicly. Explains the citizen science model, the four-tier progression, the tool, the community. This is the recruitment post designed to convert readers into PRISM Field Researchers. Includes a direct CTA to the Contribute page.
Timing: Publish week 4 or 5. After the first three posts have established credibility.

## POSTING CADENCE

Target: 1 to 2 posts per month minimum.
Major labs post 1 to 4 times per month. A dormant blog (no posts in 6+ months) is a red flag for funders.
As the contributor community grows and data flows in, posting frequency can increase.
